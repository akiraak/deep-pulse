North Seattle shoppers may soon have a new discount grocery option as WinCo Foods mulls taking over a former Sam's Club location on Aurora Avenue North.

For now, the plan is preliminary and details remain sparse.

Engineering firm JSA Civil, based in Tumwater, Thurston County, filed permit documents with the city last month noting WinCo's plans to remodel the building and reconfigure the parking lot. The remodel would include removing and rebuilding portions of existing building walls, according to the filings first reported by the Puget Sound Business Journal.

Neither WinCo nor JSA immediately returned requests for comment Tuesday. Benderson Development owns the property, along with more than 100 shopping centers across the country, and did not immediately return a request for comment. 

Beloved for its [low prices](https://www.seattletimes.com/life/food-drink/tips-to-fight-inflation-how-seattle-area-families-stretch-their-buck-at-the-grocery-store/){.content-link} and expansive bulk section, [employee-owned](https://www.wincofoods.com/about-us){.content-link .external target="_blank"} WinCo operates warehouse-style stores across the country, primarily in the West. While shoppers can find stores in Everett, Tacoma, Federal Way, Bremerton, Kent and other nearby cities, WinCo does not have a Seattle location.

The expansive vacant site at 13550 Aurora Ave. N. has attracted curiosity since Sam's Club [shuttered the store](https://www.seattletimes.com/business/sams-club-closing-3-stores-around-seattle-costing-nearly-500-jobs/){.content-link} in 2018. Costco fans buzzed in 2021 when the company filed plans to remodel the site, but that plan appeared to go dark. Costco has not filed any further permit applications.
