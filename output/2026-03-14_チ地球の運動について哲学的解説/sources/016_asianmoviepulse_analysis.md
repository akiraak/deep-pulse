# Anime Analysis: Orb: On the Movements of the Earth (2024-2025) by Kenichi Shinizu

## Overview

This anime review examines a historical epic centered on the conflict between faith and scientific progress during 15th-century Europe. The narrative spans multiple decades and follows various characters as they encounter the heliocentric model and face persecution from the Inquisition.

## Story Structure

The anime unfolds across four distinct time periods:

**First Arc (15th Century):** Centers on Rafal, a young astronomy enthusiast raised by Potocki who encounters heretic scholar Hubert and learns about heliocentrism—directly contradicting Church doctrine.

**Second Arc (Ten Years Later):** Features duelists Oczy and Gras who discover forbidden astronomical texts and join a group of scholars studying heliocentrism while evading Inquisitor Nowak.

**Third Arc (Twenty-Five Years Later):** Introduces Schmidt, captain of the Heretic Liberation Force, who leads military operations against Church forces alongside allies including Draka, a Romani woman seeking financial reform.

**Final Arc (1468):** Involves baker's assistant Albert Brudzewski in the ongoing conflict surrounding heliocentrism.

## Narrative Strengths

According to the review, the anime excels through multiple storytelling dimensions. The interconnected narrative structure maintains engagement throughout 25 episodes. The antagonist Nowak serves as a central figure whose personal suffering adds complexity beyond simple villainy.

"The discussions appearing throughout...are as deep as they are intriguing" regarding how faith, progress, and their evolution are presented as both teachings and battlegrounds.

Character development receives particular praise. Jolenta's arc is described as "probably the most dramatic in the whole anime," with compelling portrayals across the ensemble cast.

## Technical Assessment

**Strengths:** Animation quality during battle sequences proves competent, with brutal yet dramatically integrated combat. Depictions of the starry sky, particularly the Pleiades constellation, create "breathtaking" imagery that enhances thematic resonance.

**Weaknesses:** Character design by Shino Masanori produces "blunt figures" with insufficient visual distinction beyond dialogue. Background details remain sparse, and overall coloring appears uninspired except for celestial scenes.

## Critical Evaluation

The review concludes that "Orb" represents "one of the most captivating" anime experiences in recent memory. The primary limitations involve technical presentation and subject matter heaviness discouraging continuous viewing—though the reviewer questions whether the latter constitutes genuine criticism.

The final arc receives criticism for feeling underdeveloped at merely two episodes, particularly following the more elaborate previous storyline.
