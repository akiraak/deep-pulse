# Review: Orb: On the Movements of the Earth

## Overview
This is a detailed anime review from the "Anime War Crime Tribunal" blog, posted June 8, 2025. The reviewer examines the anime series "Orb: On the Movements of the Earth," which spans multiple seasons (2024 Fall and 2025 Winter).

## Key Strengths Identified

The reviewer praises the narrative's nuanced exploration of how characters perceive their world. They note the series excels at depicting geocentrism as "a highly predictive model, refined by some of the greatest minds" rather than mere dogma. The first two story arcs receive particular acclaim for their handling of intellectual curiosity and the conflict between knowledge-seeking and institutional authority.

Character development stands out, particularly the dynamic between Badeni and Oczy, which shows how individuals with opposing worldviews can influence each other positively. The presentation of Rafal's final moments and Nowak's transformation receives specific commendation.

## Criticisms

The reviewer identifies significant issues with later arcs. They find certain plot developments implausible—such as Draka convincing a bishop to publish forbidden knowledge through a chance encounter. The shift away from astronomy-focused narrative toward broader philosophical themes weakens the story's emotional resonance.

The final two episodes prove most problematic. The reviewer considers the ending "frustrating," particularly the alternate history connection to real events and the revelation about Rafal. These elements feel forced and diminish the story's impact.

## Final Assessment

**Rating: 8.5/10**

The reviewer recommends the series "with an asterisk," suggesting viewers should know quality deteriorates significantly in the final episodes.
