# Du mouvement de la Terre : de l'émotion des sciences - Comixtrip

## Overview

This page reviews "Du mouvement de la Terre" (On the Movement of the Earth), a manga by Uoto published by Ki oon. The work explores the historical conflict between scientific advancement and religious dogma during the late Middle Ages.

## Main Plot and Characters

The narrative follows **Rafal**, a twelve-year-old prodigy preparing for theological studies in 15th-century Europe. His world shifts when he encounters **Hubert**, a former prisoner imprisoned for supporting heliocentrism. As Rafal learns about the sun-centered universe model, his certainties crumble. Meanwhile, **Novak**, a ruthless inquisitor, monitors those questioning Church doctrine.

## Historical Context

The review explains how the Catholic Church enforced **Ptolemy's geocentric model**, where Earth remained immobile at the universe's center. This conflicted with later astronomers like **Copernicus**, **Galileo**, and **Kepler**, who demonstrated heliocentrism.

The manga depicts the "conflict between those upholding ancient astronomical views and modern thinkers challenging established dogma."

## Artistic Assessment

Uoto creates "an atmosphere marked by psychological tension through depicting inquisitorial terror." The artist employs close-ups emphasizing character emotions and confrontations, though sparse environmental details constitute a minor weakness. Illustrations depicting planetary movements in the geocentric model receive particular praise.
