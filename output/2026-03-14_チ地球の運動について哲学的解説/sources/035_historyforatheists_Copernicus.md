## Overview

This article examines the widespread myth that Nicolaus Copernicus delayed publishing his revolutionary heliocentric theory until his deathbed, supposedly out of fear of religious persecution by the Catholic Church. Tim O'Neill argues that the true historical story is considerably more complex and interesting than this popular narrative suggests.

## Key Topics Covered

The article is part of O'Neill's "Great Myths" series and addresses:

- The popular claim that Copernicus withheld his heliocentrism theory from publication until receiving it on his deathbed
- The "Conflict Thesis" and "Draper-White Thesis" regarding science and religion
- The actual historical timeline of Copernicus's work and its publication
- The Church's actual response to heliocentrism
- Medieval and Renaissance attitudes toward scientific theory
- Relevant figures including Cardinal Bellarmine, Galileo, Tycho Brahe, Kepler, and Nicole Oresme
