# Truth and Fiction in Orb: On the Movements of the Earth

## Overview

"Orb: On the Movements of the Earth" blends historical facts with fictional narrative to explore the clash between science and religion during the 15th century. The work examines the debate between geocentric (Earth-centered) and heliocentric (Sun-centered) models of the universe.

## Historical Accuracy

**Real Elements Include:**

- **Ptolemaic Model**: The story references Ptolemy's mathematical system explaining celestial motion, which "proposed that planets move in small circles (epicycles)" around larger ones centered on Earth.

- **Copernican Revolution**: The heliocentric model depicted in the manga reflects Nicolaus Copernicus's "De Revolutionibus Orbium Coelestium," published in 1543—a century after the manga's setting.

- **Scientific Observation**: The narrative emphasizes vision's importance, paralleling figures like Tycho Brahe (1546–1601), a renowned naked-eye observer whose precise astronomical measurements influenced later scientists.

- **15th-Century Context**: The story authentically portrays the Renaissance transitional period marked by tension between religious authority and emerging scientific inquiry, with women excluded from scientific participation.

- **Inquisitorial Threat**: The Inquisition's role reflects historical reality, where ecclesiastical courts condemned heretical ideas challenging orthodox doctrine.

## Fictional Elements

**Creative Liberties:**

- **Fictional Characters**: Protagonists Hubert and Rafal represent visionary thinkers from later eras, anachronistically placed in the 15th century.

- **Invented Artifacts**: A stone chest containing hidden research symbolizes knowledge preservation against censorship.

- **Dramatized Persecution**: While religious persecution occurred, "death sentences for heresy were rare," and "the Church often sponsored scientific studies." The narrative heightens dramatic tension beyond historical frequency.

## Core Message

The work encourages questioning authority in pursuit of truth, reflecting broader themes about resisting censorship and advancing knowledge—issues remaining relevant today.
