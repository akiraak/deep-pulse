The **[*Dictionnaire de l\'Académie française*]{title="French-language text"}** ([French pronunciation:]{.IPA-label .IPA-label-small} [[\[diksjɔnɛːʁ[ ]{.wrap}də[ ]{.wrap}lakademi[ ]{.wrap}fʁɑ̃sɛːz\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/French "Help:IPA/French")]{.IPA .nowrap lang="fr-Latn-fonipa"}; English: **Dictionary of the French academy**) is a [French language](/wiki/French_language "French language") [dictionary](/wiki/Dictionary "Dictionary") published by the [[[Académie française](/wiki/Acad%C3%A9mie_fran%C3%A7aise "Académie française"){.mw-redirect}]{lang="fr" style="font-style: normal;"}]{title="French-language text"}.

The [[[Académie française](/wiki/Acad%C3%A9mie_fran%C3%A7aise "Académie française"){.mw-redirect}]{lang="fr" style="font-style: normal;"}]{title="French-language text"} is an institution tasked with establishing rules for the use of the French language, the compilation of a dictionary being one of its primary tasks.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}1[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-1)^ Although it makes recommendations for the correct use of French, they carry no legal power, and are frequently disregarded, including by government authorities. The ninth edition was completed in November 2024, and is available online in its entirety along with all previous editions.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}2[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-:0-2)^

## Publication {#Publication}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Dictionnaire_de_l%27Acad%C3%A9mie_fran%C3%A7aise&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Publication")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

A special Commission (*Commission du dictionnaire*) composed of several (but not all) of the members of the Académie undertakes the compilation of the dictionary. It has published thirteen editions of the dictionary, of which three were preliminary, eight were complete, and two were supplements for specialised words.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}3[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-3)^ The completed edition of the [*Dictionnaire de l\'Académie française*]{title="French-language text"}, the first official dictionary of the French language, was presented upon completion by the Académie to King [Louis XIV](/wiki/Louis_XIV "Louis XIV").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}4[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-4)^ on 24 August 1694.

In 1778, the Académie attempted to compile a \"historical dictionary\" of the French language. The project was later abandoned, having failed to progress beyond the letter \"A\".

Work on the ninth edition started in 1986, and was concluded in 2024 by the publication of its last volume. It was published in four volumes starting in 1992:

-   1st volume (*A* to *Enzyme*), 1992 [([ISBN](/wiki/ISBN_(identifier) "ISBN (identifier)"){.mw-redirect} [978-2-11081-249-0](/wiki/Special:BookSources/978-2-11081-249-0 "Special:BookSources/978-2-11081-249-0"))]{.small}
-   2nd volume (*Éocène* to *Mappemonde*), 2000 [([ISBN](/wiki/ISBN_(identifier) "ISBN (identifier)"){.mw-redirect} [978-2-21362-143-2](/wiki/Special:BookSources/978-2-21362-143-2 "Special:BookSources/978-2-21362-143-2"))]{.small}
-   3rd volume (*Maquereau* to *Quotité*), 2011 [([ISBN](/wiki/ISBN_(identifier) "ISBN (identifier)"){.mw-redirect} [978-2-21366-640-2](/wiki/Special:BookSources/978-2-21366-640-2 "Special:BookSources/978-2-21366-640-2"))]{.small}
-   4th volume (*R* to *Zzz*), 2024 [([ISBN](/wiki/ISBN_(identifier) "ISBN (identifier)"){.mw-redirect} [978-2-213-72741-7](/wiki/Special:BookSources/978-2-213-72741-7 "Special:BookSources/978-2-213-72741-7"))]{.small}

The completed ninth edition was presented by the Académie Française to French president [Emmanuel Macron](/wiki/Emmanuel_Macron "Emmanuel Macron") on 14 November 2024.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}5[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-5)^ With almost 53,000 words, it contains 21,000 new words compared to the 8th edition.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}2[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-:0-2)^ Given that, by the time all volumes were completed, the first volume was already 32 years old and thus long out of date, the Académie published addenda of 189 words which had not been added to their respective volumes at the time of their publication.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}6[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-6)^

In part due to the unusually slow publication cycle of the [*Dictionnaire de l\'Académie Française*]{title="French-language text"}, other dictionaries (such as those published by [Larousse](/wiki/%C3%89ditions_Larousse "Éditions Larousse") and [Le Robert](/wiki/Dictionnaires_Le_Robert "Dictionnaires Le Robert")) are more commonly used as everyday reference sources.^\[*[[citation\ needed]{title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2025)"}](/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")*\]^ They are also more comprehensive, with the [*Grand Robert*]{title="French-language text"} counting 350,000 words.
