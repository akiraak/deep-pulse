The ***Deutsches Wörterbuch*** ([German:]{.IPA-label .IPA-label-small} [[\[ˌdɔʏtʃəs[ ]{.wrap}ˈvœʁtɐbuːx\]](/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German")]{.IPA .nowrap lang="de-Latn-fonipa"}; \"German Dictionary\"), abbreviated ***DWB***, is the largest and most comprehensive [dictionary](/wiki/Dictionary "Dictionary") of the [German language](/wiki/German_language "German language") in existence.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}1[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-BBAWsynopsis-1)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}2[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-harvard-2)^ Encompassing modern [High German](/wiki/High_German_languages "High German languages") vocabulary in use since 1450, it also includes [loanwords](/wiki/Loanword "Loanword") adopted from other languages into German. Entries cover the [etymology](/wiki/Etymology "Etymology"), [meanings](/wiki/Meaning_(linguistics) "Meaning (linguistics)"){.mw-redirect}, [attested forms](/wiki/Attested_form "Attested form"){.mw-redirect}, [synonyms](/wiki/Synonym "Synonym"), usage peculiarities, and regional differences of words found throughout the [German speaking world](/wiki/German_speaking_world "German speaking world"){.mw-redirect}. The dictionary\'s [historical linguistics](/wiki/Historical_linguistics "Historical linguistics") approach, illuminated by examples from [primary source](/wiki/Primary_source "Primary source") documents, makes it to German what the *[Oxford English Dictionary](/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary "Oxford English Dictionary")* is to [English](/wiki/English_language "English language").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}3[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-trier-3)^ The first completed DWB lists over 330,000 [headwords](/wiki/Headword "Headword"){.mw-redirect} in 67,000 print columns spanning 32 volumes.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}4[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-BBAWdesc-4)^

The *Deutsches Wörterbuch* was begun by the [Brothers Grimm](/wiki/Brothers_Grimm "Brothers Grimm") in 1838 and the initial volumes were published in 1854. Unfinished at the time of their deaths, the dictionary was finally completed by a succession of later scholars and institutions in 1961.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}1[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-BBAWsynopsis-1)^ In 1971, a 33rd supplement volume was published containing 25,000 additional entries. New research projects began in 2004 to expand and update the oldest parts of the dictionary to modern academic standards. Volumes A--F were planned for completion in 2012 by the Language Research Centre at the [Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities](/wiki/Berlin-Brandenburg_Academy_of_Sciences_and_Humanities "Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities")^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}4[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-BBAWdesc-4)^ and the [University of Göttingen](/wiki/University_of_G%C3%B6ttingen "University of Göttingen").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}2[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-harvard-2)^

### Beginnings {#Beginnings}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_W%C3%B6rterbuch&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Beginnings")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

Beginning in 1830, [Weidmann\'s Publishing House](/wiki/Weidmannsche_Buchhandlung "Weidmannsche Buchhandlung") in [Leipzig](/wiki/Leipzig "Leipzig") repeatedly approached [Jacob](/wiki/Jacob_Grimm "Jacob Grimm") and [Wilhelm Grimm](/wiki/Wilhelm_Grimm "Wilhelm Grimm") with a proposal for a large new dictionary, spanning [German](/wiki/German_language "German language") vocabulary from [Martin Luther](/wiki/Martin_Luther "Martin Luther") to [Johann Wolfgang von Goethe](/wiki/Johann_Wolfgang_von_Goethe "Johann Wolfgang von Goethe"). As busy professors at [Göttingen University](/wiki/G%C3%B6ttingen_University "Göttingen University"){.mw-redirect}, the [Brothers Grimm](/wiki/Brothers_Grimm "Brothers Grimm") rejected such a complex undertaking. A political scandal then dramatically changed matters. In 1837, the new King of Hanover, [Ernst August](/wiki/Ernest_Augustus,_King_of_Hanover "Ernest Augustus, King of Hanover"), dissolved parliament and demanded oaths of allegiance from all civil servants. The brothers and five other professors refused and this so-called \"[Göttingen Seven](/wiki/G%C3%B6ttingen_Seven "Göttingen Seven")\" were removed from their positions by royal order. The brothers then became political refugees in their former home in [Kassel](/wiki/Kassel "Kassel").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}5[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-5)^

### []{#The_Grimms_.281838.E2.80.931863.29}The Grimms (1838--1863) {#The_Grimms_(1838–1863)}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_W%C3%B6rterbuch&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: The Grimms (1838–1863)")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

By October 1838, the Brothers had a contract with Weidmann\'s and the prestigious Leipzig journal *[Allgemeine Zeitung](/wiki/Allgemeine_Zeitung "Allgemeine Zeitung")* published an announcement of the start of work on the DWB. The brothers initially expected the project to take ten years and produce 6--7 volumes. Circumstances seemed favorable because they were provided with staff and spacious apartments in [Berlin](/wiki/Berlin "Berlin") at the invitation of the Prussian Minister in 1841. However, difficulties soon began. Not only did the acquisition of source excerpts take much longer than expected, but illness and the [Revolution of 1848](/wiki/Revolutions_of_1848_in_the_German_states "Revolutions of 1848 in the German states"){.mw-redirect} interrupted the work. Eight volumes consisting of 1824 printed columns, a bibliography and a detailed preface were finally published on 13 April 1854.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}6[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-DWBhistory-6)^

The first edition of the *DWB* exceeded the expectations of the brothers and the publishers. The press called it a \"great national work\" and its first shipments sold 10,000 copies. As it included words regarded as \"indelicate,\" Jacob anticipated criticism of this and stated the following in the Foreword:

> This dictionary is not an immoral book, but a scientific undertaking. Even the [Bible](/wiki/Bible "Bible") does not lack for words that are frowned upon in fine society.

--- [Jacob Grimm](/wiki/Jacob_Grimm "Jacob Grimm"), *Vorwort 1. Band, S. XXXIV*, Leipzig 1854[]{plural="1" style="display:none"}

More volumes and updates were planned, but in their lifetime the brothers could only fully complete portions: Wilhelm Grimm wrote the articles to the letter D and died in 1859; Jacob, who was able to fully complete the letters A, B, C and E, died in 1863 while working on the entry for \"Frucht\" (fruit).^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}7[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-7)^

### []{#Post-Grimm_era_.281863.E2.80.931907.29}Post-Grimm era (1863--1907) {#Post-Grimm_era_(1863–1907)}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_W%C3%B6rterbuch&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Post-Grimm era (1863–1907)")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

After the deaths of the Grimm Brothers, successive linguists continued the work. The first of these were close associates of the brothers, [Rudolf Hildebrand](/wiki/Rudolf_Hildebrand "Rudolf Hildebrand") and [Karl Weigand](/w/index.php?title=Karl_Weigand&action=edit&redlink=1 "Karl Weigand (page does not exist)"){.new}. The *DWB* also became an affair of state when [Otto von Bismarck](/wiki/Otto_von_Bismarck "Otto von Bismarck") requested the [North German Confederation](/wiki/North_German_Confederation "North German Confederation") Federal Council to provide state funding in 1867. The young [Germanist](/wiki/Germanist "Germanist"){.mw-redirect} [Moritz Heyne](/wiki/Moritz_Heyne "Moritz Heyne") joined the project and became one of its most important contributors. By 1888 Heyne had invited graduate students to post articles under his supervision, turning the DWB into a true consortium for the first time. Included in this group was Rudolf Meißner who collaborated on the *DWB* for six decades (1889--1948). These ever-changing authors had different approaches and the work also proceeded very slowly. Hermann Wunderlich, Hildebrand\'s successor, only finished *Gestüme* to *Gezwang* after 20 years of work and 3000 columns of text. By 1905, academic professionals across Germany were unanimous: management of the DWB had to change or it would never be completed.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}6[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-DWBhistory-6)^

### []{#Academy_of_Sciences_.281908.E2.80.931961.29}Academy of Sciences (1908--1961) {#Academy_of_Sciences_(1908–1961)}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_W%C3%B6rterbuch&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Academy of Sciences (1908–1961)")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

The prestigious [Prussian Academy of Sciences](/wiki/Prussian_Academy_of_Sciences "Prussian Academy of Sciences") took on formal development of the *DWB* in 1908 with [Göttingen](/wiki/G%C3%B6ttingen "Göttingen") remaining a central collection point for [source documents](/wiki/Source_document "Source document"). Operations were streamlined and salaried workers were provided through funding by the Empire. This period of reform and consolidation ended with [World War I](/wiki/World_War_I "World War I") in 1914. By the early \'20s the project was again close to collapse as [German hyperinflation](/wiki/Hyperinflation_in_the_Weimar_Republic "Hyperinflation in the Weimar Republic") drove costs of production to over 5 billion marks. A donation of just \$152 from the United States in 1923 saved the *DWB* from ruin. [Max Planck](/wiki/Max_Planck "Max Planck") repeatedly advocated for the dictionary and funding was eventually taken up by the [Emergency Association of German Science](/wiki/Emergency_Association_of_German_Science "Emergency Association of German Science"){.mw-redirect}. Due to the efficiencies of a permanent staff of lexicographers as well as standardized policies for production, the period between 1931--1939 saw six times as much work completed as in the previous years. Nearly 100 years after its conception the DWB was permanently institutionalized and its conclusion was in sight.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}6[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-DWBhistory-6)^

[World War II](/wiki/World_War_II "World War II") then paralyzed the work. Employees were drafted into military service, resources were scarce, and the archived holdings were moved to a [potash mine](/w/index.php?title=Potash_mine&action=edit&redlink=1 "Potash mine (page does not exist)"){.new} at [Bernburg an der Saale](/wiki/Bernburg "Bernburg") to protect them from [Allied bombing](/wiki/Allied_bombing_of_Berlin_in_World_War_II "Allied bombing of Berlin in World War II"){.mw-redirect}. The remaining three employees continued some work at Schloss Fredersdorf outside Berlin. After the war the USSR granted permission to move the archived materials from Fredersdorf and Bernburg back to Berlin in 1947. The new [German Academy of Sciences at Berlin](/wiki/German_Academy_of_Sciences_at_Berlin "German Academy of Sciences at Berlin") then took on the work of the DWB. With the founding of [East Germany](/wiki/East_Germany "East Germany") in 1949, communication between Berlin and Göttingen became more difficult. Despite these political complications, the *DWB* was finally published in January 1961, 123 years after its beginnings. It totaled 67,744 text columns, 320,000 keywords and weighed 84 kg. A 1971 supplement contains 25,000 additional entries and references to primary sources.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}3[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-trier-3)^

## Later versions {#Later_versions}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_W%C3%B6rterbuch&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Later versions")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

Planning for a second edition had already begun in 1957, four years before the first edition was finished. The revision was intended especially to bring the oldest part of the *Dictionary*, the letters A--F originally authored by the [Brothers Grimm](/wiki/Brothers_Grimm "Brothers Grimm"), up to date. It also was to be a cooperative effort between [East](/wiki/East_Germany "East Germany") and [West Germany](/wiki/West_Germany "West Germany"): the [Academy of Sciences of the GDR](/wiki/Academy_of_Sciences_of_the_GDR "Academy of Sciences of the GDR"){.mw-redirect} in [East Berlin](/wiki/East_Berlin "East Berlin") would complete A--C, and the [University of Göttingen](/wiki/University_of_G%C3%B6ttingen "University of Göttingen") in the West would complete D--F. The initial fascicles of this revision were published in 1965, but it remains unfinished today, with the letters B and C (originally assigned to the Berlin team) still in progress. The East German Berlin team was greatly hindered for political reasons because the [SED](/wiki/Socialist_Unity_Party_of_Germany "Socialist Unity Party of Germany") authorities saw the DWB as a \"bourgeois\" lexicography project. In the course of the 1960s, most of its staff were taken off the project and used for other tasks.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}8[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-8)^ In 1984, the original 1961 version of the *DWB* was published in a paperback edition, now out of print. In 1999 a new paperback printing of all 33 volumes (weight 30 kg), published by Deutscher Taschenbuch Verlag, became available ([ISBN](/wiki/ISBN_(identifier) "ISBN (identifier)"){.mw-redirect} [978-3423590457](/wiki/Special:BookSources/978-3423590457 "Special:BookSources/978-3423590457")).

## Digital edition {#Digital_edition}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_W%C3%B6rterbuch&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Digital edition")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

In 2004 the Competence Center for Electronic Text Processing and Publication in the Humanities at the [University of Trier](/wiki/University_of_Trier "University of Trier") digitized the entire 300 million printed characters according to the method of double entry. The entire body was manually entered twice in China to eliminate error.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}9[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-9)^ A set of CD-ROMs of this digitization was released for Microsoft Windows, Linux and Mac OS. In this version spelling errors in the original were corrected. An online version of the first edition is also available at the [University of Trier](/wiki/University_of_Trier "University of Trier").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}3[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-trier-3)^ The digitized first edition of the *DWB* met with lively interest. Every day the online version receives tens of thousands of hits and the CD-ROM of the first edition is now in its fourth updated distribution.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}10[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-10)^

## New edition {#New_edition}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=Deutsches_W%C3%B6rterbuch&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: New edition")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

In 2006 the unfinished project to revise and update the A--F volumes to modern academic standards was resumed. The conclusion of this work (the B and C volumes) was finished in 2016;^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}11[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-11)^ fascicles are appearing with the S. Hirzel Verlag ^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}12[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-12)^ as they are completed.

However, the [Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities](/wiki/Berlin-Brandenburg_Academy_of_Sciences_and_Humanities "Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities")^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}13[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-BBAWprojects-13)^ announced that no revision of the volumes G to Z is planned. According to the Academic Director of the Berlin-Brandenburg Academy, Wolf-Hagen Krauth, the sheer labor that would be required exceeds the possibilities for funding it in today\'s world.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}14[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-14)^
