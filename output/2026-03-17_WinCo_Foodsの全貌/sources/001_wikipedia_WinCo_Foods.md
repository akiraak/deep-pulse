**WinCo Foods, Inc.** is a [privately held](/wiki/Privately_held_company "Privately held company") American [supermarket](/wiki/Supermarket "Supermarket") chain based in [Boise, Idaho](/wiki/Boise,_Idaho "Boise, Idaho"). WinCo is majority [employee-owned](/wiki/Employee_stock_ownership "Employee stock ownership"),^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}5[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-SN2013-06-5)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}6[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-6)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}7[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-grocery-7)^ and has retail stores in [Arizona](/wiki/Arizona "Arizona"), [California](/wiki/California "California"), [Idaho](/wiki/Idaho "Idaho"), [Montana](/wiki/Montana "Montana"), [Nevada](/wiki/Nevada "Nevada"), [Oklahoma](/wiki/Oklahoma "Oklahoma"), [Oregon](/wiki/Oregon "Oregon"), [Texas](/wiki/Texas "Texas"),^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}8[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-8)^ [Utah](/wiki/Utah "Utah"), and [Washington](/wiki/Washington_(state) "Washington (state)"). It was founded in 1967 as a no-frills [warehouse-style store](/wiki/Warehouse_store "Warehouse store") with low prices. The stores feature extensive [bulk food](/wiki/Bulk_foods "Bulk foods") sections.

Until 1998, it operated as Waremart and [Cub Foods](/wiki/Cub_Foods "Cub Foods"){.mw-redirect}, the latter under a [franchise agreement](/wiki/Franchise_agreement "Franchise agreement"). However, WinCo began re-establishing Waremart Foods in 2017. As of 2022^[\[update\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=WinCo_Foods&action=edit){.external .text}^, WinCo has 138 [retail stores](/wiki/Retail_stores "Retail stores"){.mw-redirect} and six [distribution centers](/wiki/Distribution_centers "Distribution centers"){.mw-redirect}, with over 20,000 employees.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}1[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-WinCo_Corporate_Site-1)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}9[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-9)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}10[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-10)^ As of November 2024, WinCo Foods was No. 53 in [Forbes.com](/wiki/Forbes "Forbes")\'s list of the largest privately owned companies in the United States.

## Overview {#Overview}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=WinCo_Foods&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Overview")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

WinCo Foods is based in [Boise, Idaho](/wiki/Boise,_Idaho "Boise, Idaho"). It was founded in 1967, and the company is mostly owned by current and former employees through an [employee stock ownership plan](/wiki/Employee_stock_ownership_plan "Employee stock ownership plan"){.mw-redirect}.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}11[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-j958-11)^ WinCo operates distribution centers in the following locations:^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}1[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-WinCo_Corporate_Site-1)^

-   [Woodburn, Oregon](/wiki/Woodburn,_Oregon "Woodburn, Oregon")
-   [Myrtle Creek, Oregon](/wiki/Myrtle_Creek,_Oregon "Myrtle Creek, Oregon")
-   [Boise, Idaho](/wiki/Boise,_Idaho "Boise, Idaho")
-   [Phoenix, Arizona](/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona "Phoenix, Arizona")
-   [Denton, Texas](/wiki/Denton,_Texas "Denton, Texas")
-   [Modesto, California](/wiki/Modesto,_California "Modesto, California")

The company reduces [operating expenses](/wiki/Operating_expense "Operating expense") by [purchasing](/wiki/Purchasing "Purchasing") directly from manufacturers and farmers, operating basic no-frills stores, and not providing a [bagging service](/wiki/Bagger "Bagger").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}12[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-12)^ In addition, the company does not accept credit cards for payment due to transaction fees (debit and [WIC](/wiki/WIC_program "WIC program")/[EBT](/wiki/Electronic_benefit_transfer "Electronic benefit transfer") cards are accepted).^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}13[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-13)^

## History {#History}

[[\[]{.mw-editsection-bracket}[edit](/w/index.php?title=WinCo_Foods&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: History")[\]]{.mw-editsection-bracket}]{.mw-editsection}

The company, originally called Waremart, was founded in Boise, Idaho, in 1967 by Ralph Ward and Bud Williams as a no-frills, [warehouse-style grocery store](/wiki/Warehouse_store "Warehouse store") focusing on [low prices](/wiki/Discount_store "Discount store").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}7[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-grocery-7)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}14[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-history-14)^ In 1985, Waremart employees established an [employee stock ownership plan](/wiki/Employee_ownership "Employee ownership"){.mw-redirect} and purchased a majority stake of Waremart from the Ward family, making the company employee-owned.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}7[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-grocery-7)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}14[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-history-14)^

In January 1991, Waremart opened an 82,000-square-foot (7,600-square-meter) store in Boise to replace the two older Boise stores.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}15[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-largest-15)^ At the time, Waremart was operating 16 stores in the [Northwest](/wiki/Northwestern_United_States "Northwestern United States") and had reported annual sales of more than \$300 million.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}15[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-largest-15)^

### WinCo Foods {#WinCo_Foods}

In October 1998, Waremart changed its name to WinCo Foods, citing confusion with retailers [Kmart](/wiki/Kmart "Kmart") and [Walmart](/wiki/Walmart "Walmart") as reason for the new name.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}16[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-name_change-16)^ The name is a [portmanteau](/wiki/Portmanteau "Portmanteau") of \"winning company\".^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}16[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-name_change-16)^ Three stores in Oregon are branded as \"Waremart by WinCo\" (those in [Independence](/wiki/Independence,_Oregon "Independence, Oregon"), [Keizer](/wiki/Keizer,_Oregon "Keizer, Oregon"), and [Ontario](/wiki/Ontario,_Oregon "Ontario, Oregon")).

There is a popular but erroneous idea that the company name is an acronym consisting of the first letters of the company\'s original five states of operation: Washington, Idaho, Nevada, California, and Oregon. Michael Read, WinCo\'s VP of Public and Legal Affairs as of 2012, called the theory \"part of the folklore\".^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}17[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-17)\[*[[AI-retrieved\ source]{title="The source near this tag was retrieved by an AI model and needs to be reviewed. (March 2026)"}](/wiki/Template:AI-retrieved_source/doc "Template:AI-retrieved source/doc")*\]^

In 2007, WinCo Foods accused a competing chain, [Save Mart](/wiki/Save_Mart "Save Mart"){.mw-redirect}, of directing a lawsuit filed by a neighborhood group *Tracy First* of [Tracy, California](/wiki/Tracy,_California "Tracy, California"), to oppose city approval of a WinCo store. That same year, WinCo Foods opened in [Pittsburg, California](/wiki/Pittsburg,_California "Pittsburg, California").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}18[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-sue-18)^

In early 2009, WinCo opened its first two stores in the [Spokane, Washington](/wiki/Spokane,_Washington "Spokane, Washington"), area.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}19[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-19)^ In October, 2009, WinCo expanded to Utah, adding two stores in [West Valley City](/wiki/West_Valley_City,_Utah "West Valley City, Utah") and [Midvale](/wiki/Midvale,_Utah "Midvale, Utah").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}14[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-history-14)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}20[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-20)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}21[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-21)^ An additional Utah store opened in [Roy](/wiki/Roy,_Utah "Roy, Utah") on June 28, 2010.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}22[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-22)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}23[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-23)^ bringing the total number of stores expanded to Utah to five.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}24[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-24)^ WinCo previously operated stores in Utah under the Waremart banner prior to the company\'s name change.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}14[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-history-14)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}25[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-25)^

In January 2011, WinCo began signing leases for an expansion to Southern Nevada and Arizona.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}26[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-26)^ The chain opened stores in [Las Vegas](/wiki/Las_Vegas "Las Vegas") and [Henderson, Nevada](/wiki/Henderson,_Nevada "Henderson, Nevada"), on March 4, 2012.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}27[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-27)^ The company\'s first two stores in Arizona opened on April 1, 2012, in the [Phoenix](/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona "Phoenix, Arizona") area.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}28[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-28)^ The company opened multiple locations in Texas, primarily in the [Dallas--Fort Worth](/wiki/Dallas%E2%80%93Fort_Worth_metroplex "Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex") area, beginning in 2014 after it completed a distribution center in the area.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}29[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-29)[[\[]{.cite-bracket}30[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-30)^

WinCo was named as the sponsor for the [WinCo Foods Portland Open](/wiki/WinCo_Foods_Portland_Open "WinCo Foods Portland Open") in June 2013.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}31[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-pga-31)^

In late 2014, WinCo announced that it would enter the [Oklahoma City metro](/wiki/Oklahoma_City_metropolitan_area "Oklahoma City metropolitan area") market, starting with stores in [Moore](/wiki/Moore,_Oklahoma "Moore, Oklahoma") and [Midwest City](/wiki/Midwest_City,_Oklahoma "Midwest City, Oklahoma"), with plans to open two other locations.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}32[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-32)^

In May 2018, Grant Haag was made president and CEO of WinCo Foods.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}33[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-33)^

Two alleged shoplifters sued WinCo alleging excessive force during separate incidents at the [Happy Valley, Oregon](/wiki/Happy_Valley,_Oregon "Happy Valley, Oregon") store.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}34[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-34)^ There were also allegations of excessive force used against a teenage girl accused of shoplifting at their [Vancouver, Washington](/wiki/Vancouver,_Washington "Vancouver, Washington"), store in 2017.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}35[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-35)^

WinCo paid a class action settlement of \$3.6 million in 2023. It was alleged that WinCo stores in [Portland, Oregon](/wiki/Portland,_Oregon "Portland, Oregon"), charged a hidden clean energy surcharge on non-grocery items.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}36[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-36)^

In October 2024, engineering firm JSA Civil filed a proposal for the first WinCo Foods location in Seattle.^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}37[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-37)^

In 2025, WinCo announced plans to expand into [Colorado](/wiki/Colorado "Colorado") with stores earmarked for [Thornton](/wiki/Thornton,_Colorado "Thornton, Colorado"), [Firestone](/wiki/Firestone,_Colorado "Firestone, Colorado"), and [Loveland](/wiki/Loveland,_Colorado "Loveland, Colorado").^[[\[]{.cite-bracket}38[\]]{.cite-bracket}](#cite_note-38)^
