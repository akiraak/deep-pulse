[William D. Long, the former chairman and CEO of WinCo Foods who ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[[led the transition of the company](https://boisedev.com/news/2025/08/18/bill-long-winco/){.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_link rel="noreferrer" target="_blank"}]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[ to employee ownership, has died, according to a report on BoiseDev.com. He was 87.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

[Long began working at WinCo in 1968 when it operated a single store, then known as Waremart, in Boise, Idaho. He became president in 1978 and CEO in 1985, and that year led the acquisition of the company by its employees from its founding family.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

[He ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[[retired as CEO in 2006](/finance/winco-s-long-retires-goddard-named-ceo){.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_link discover="true" target="_self"}]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[ but continued to serve as non-executive chairman for several years. The company, which remains employee-owned, now operates more than 140 stores across several Western states. ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

["Bill was more than an accomplished businessman--he was a visionary whose energy, integrity and commitment shaped WinCo from its inception," said Grant Haag, the current CEO of WinCo Foods, in a memo to employees that was provided to ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[[Supermarket News]{.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_italic}]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[. "His belief in employee ownership, his dedication to building strong communities, and his leadership in guiding WinCo through its formative years remain central to both our identity and success."]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

### [WinCo buyout ushered in era of growth]{.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_bold} {#WinCo buyout ushered in era of growth .ContentText .ContentText_variant_h3 .ContentText_align_left testid="content-text" style="scroll-margin-top:10rem;scroll-snap-margin-top:10rem"}

[WinCo Foods is the ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[[second-largest employee-owned company](https://www.nceo.org/research/employee-ownership-100-largest-employee-owned-companies){.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_link rel="noreferrer" target="_blank"}]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[ in the U.S., behind only Publix Super Markets, according to the National Center for Employee Ownership. The NCEO listed WinCo as having 22,647 employees. The company had ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[[2024 revenues of \$9.8 billion](https://www.forbes.com/companies/winco-foods/){.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_link rel="noreferrer" target="_blank"}]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[, according to Forbes.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

[WinCo was founded in 1967 as a no-frills, warehouse-style store by Boise-area businessmen Ralph Ward and Bud Williams. Ward bought out Williams a few years later. After Ward died in 1985, Long and other employees bought a controlling stake in the company from the surviving members of Ward's family.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

["Bill Long gathered a group of managers and supervisors together in the warehouse, while a number of people sat on crates and boxes, and they laid out a plan and signed a letter agreeing to work together to gain a controlling stake in the company and operate the stores," WinCo ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[[said in a video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wX-q5j2MhCk){.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_link rel="noreferrer" target="_blank"}]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[ about its history on its YouTube channel.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

[The employees formed an Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP) in 1990, which gave every employee of the company an ownership stake. The company operated 17 stores in five Western states at that time.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

[The employee-owners continued to drive the company's bare-bones operating model, often repairing their own refrigeration units and shopping carts, and splitting erasers in half to share them and save money, according to the video.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

["This philosophy of low cost, minimal frills, and employee ownership became the culture of Waremart," the video said.]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

[The company expanded rapidly under Long's leadership. It changed its name to WinCo in 1999, although it still operates three stores under the Waremart banner in Oregon, according to its website. (WinCo stands for Winning Company, although it also represents the initials of the five states it operated in at the time of the employee buyout---Washington, Idaho, Nevada, California, and Oregon.)]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}

[WinCo, which is currently ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[[celebrating its 40th year](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NH2UIfmqjNg){.ContentText-BodyTextChunk .ContentText-BodyTextChunk_link rel="noreferrer" target="_blank"}]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}[ as an employee-owned company, recently reported that the average ESOP balance for employees who have been with the company for 30 years or more is more than \$3 million. ]{.ContentText .ContentText_variant_bodyNormal testid="content-text"}
