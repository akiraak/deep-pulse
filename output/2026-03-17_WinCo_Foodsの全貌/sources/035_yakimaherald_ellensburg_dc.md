Ellensburg could potentially see a five-parcel, 1 million-square-foot grocery distribution center west of Anderson Road.

WinCo Foods Inc., a privately held and majority employee-owned U.S. supermarket chain based out of Boise, Idaho, is looking to establish a distribution center within the city's limits, according to documents submitted to the city of Ellensburg.

The proposed facility is logistical in nature and won't provide for consumer shopping.

The proposed development would sit on over 100 acres and would include an over 1.1 million-square-foot grocery distribution warehouse, a roughly 47,000-square-foot truck returns warehouse, a nearly 800-square-foot driver waiting building and an almost 400-square-foot check-in building, according to documents submitted to the city.

The city had a public hearing for a height variance May 11, which was approved.

The project site is west of Anderson Road and east of Interstate 90 and has historically been used for hay farming and hay storage by the Anderson Hay & Grain Company, according to [documents submitted to the city](https://ci.ellensburg.wa.us/DocumentCenter/View/17746/-Height-Variance-Narrative?bidId=){target="_blank"}.

The distribution warehouse would store dry goods, frozen foods and refrigerated foods for distribution to WinCo Foods stores throughout the Pacific Northwest. The building would have a mechanized warehouse automation system to sort and move items.

Ellensburg Planning Manager Jeremy Johnston says the developers can now proceed with a boundary adjustment application and then apply for a building permit.

The boundary application would have to be processed before or concurrently with the building permit, Johnston said.

There is currently no timeline for completion of the facility, he said.
